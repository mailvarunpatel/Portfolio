About

Building at the intersection of hardware, software & strategy.

I've spent my career in connectivity and technology — from deploying carrier-grade networks across Africa to leading product strategy for global IoT, satellite, and cloud platforms. What sets me apart is experience across both hardware and software product development — a combination that's rare in product management today.

At emnify, I own product strategy and roadmap for satellite connectivity, core network signaling, MVNO/MVNE PaaS, and eSIM enablement — presenting directly to C-level stakeholders. I've enabled MNOs onto the platform driving significant new ARR, launched 5G NSA capabilities, and brought satellite IoT connectivity to Brazil — opening deployments in previously unreachable environments.

At LYNX Technik, I owned the go-to-market launch of the world's first real-time, frame-by-frame HDR-to-SDR converter — a product that won both the NAB Product of the Year and IBC Best of Show awards. I coordinated with engineering and university research partners, created demo videos and marketing materials, and represented the product at major industry exhibitions.

Before that, I joined Willowmore as the first product employee and co-invented a patented IoT smart padlock combining BLE and cellular connectivity. I managed OEM hardware and software partners in China, defined UX for web and mobile applications, and took the product from concept through pilots across six countries to the company's first enterprise sale — a seven-figure enterprise deal with no prior customer references.

What energizes me most today is operating at the intersection of product strategy and organizational impact — leading and mentoring PM teams, setting product direction at a broader level, and building platforms that create lasting competitive advantage.

Broadcast Tech · LYNX Technik · World's First

World's First Real-Time "Frame-by-Frame" HDR to SDR Converter

Challenge
Broadcasters needed real-time HDR to SDR conversion for live events — but existing solutions used static corrections that failed in unpredictable lighting conditions. The product was in development with a university partner, but needed a complete go-to-market strategy to reach the broadcast industry.
Approach
Owned the full GTM lifecycle: coordinated with engineering and the university research team, created product demo videos and presentation materials, built marketing strategy and documentation with external partners, and represented the product at major industry exhibitions including NAB and IBC.
Result
Successfully launched the world's first automated frame-by-frame dynamic HDR to SDR converter to market. Won both the NAB Product of the Year Award and IBC Best of Show.
